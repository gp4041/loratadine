This project is an analysis of weekly Google searches for Loratadine, a medical drug used to relieve symptoms of hay fever. The data was taken from Google Trends. The analysis will look at how to best measure the trend of the Time Series, displays of seasonality and using the prophet function to forecast how Loratadine will trend throughout 2025.

This project included the installation of the prophet and readxl packages.

Here, linear, quadratic and cubic models have been fitted to the data using the lm function. Looking at their respective summaries, the cubic fit has the largest R-squared, 0.0575. This is very low indicating that none of these models are suitable fits for the data. Looking at their respective plots further supports this. This suggests that using parametric methods to estimate the trend is not a good idea.

Calculate 4 point moving average.

loratadineAv4 <- stats::filter(loratadineDf$y, c(1/4, 1/4, 1/4, 1/4))
plot(loratadineDf$ds, loratadineDf$y, type ="o", xlab="Week", ylab="Trends")
lines(loratadineDf$ds, loratadineAv4, type="l", col="red", lwd=2)

A four point moving average of the data has been assigned to loratadineAv4. Plotting as a line against the original data shows that this is a much better representation of the trend than the regression models.

Spencer’s 15-point moving average

Sp = signal::spencerFilter()
loratadineSp <- stats::filter(loratadineDf$y, Sp)
plot(loratadineDf$ds, loratadineDf$y, type ="o", xlab="Week", ylab="Trends")
lines(loratadineDf$ds, loratadineSp, type="l", col="red", lwd=2)

In this part, Spencer’s 15-point moving average has been applied to the data. With this filter, the seasonal peaks have been reduced in comparison to the 4-point moving average.

First, a time series variation of the data was made so that it could be analysed. Decomposing the data and looking at the respective plots, there is a very clear seasonal trend with the searches of Loratadine. When looking at the seasonal components, they are negative for most of the year, then for a couple weeks in summer the shoot upwards, reaching a peak of 49.104. This further tells us that interest in Loratadine is subdued throughout the majority of the year while its interest surges briefly in the summer months. Again, this corresponds to when experiences with hayfeaver are most pronounced.

The component plot also shows that there has been an upwards trend in the interest of Loratadine year on year, alongside clear seasonality.
